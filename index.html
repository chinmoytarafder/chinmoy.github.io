Decoupling Moments of an Oblique Wing Aircraft to Develop Control Algorithms

Conducted extensive research and testing to develop control algorithms by decoupling moments of an oblique wing aircraft, including:

  • Phase 1 testing in a 2'x2' wind tunnel with a straight wing at varying sweep angles
  • Designing and building a model aircraft mirroring the phase 1 wing's aspect ratio with integrated control surfaces and a rotating mechanism
  • Testing the model in a 5' x 7' wind tunnel, collecting data on sweep angles and control surface deflections
  • Conducting CFD simulations to supplement experimental data
  • Using statistical software (JMP) to analyze data and create equations for aircraft stabilization via control surface adjustments
